medical monitoring project
Project Requirement: Intelligent Monitoring Device and System for Hospital Urine Drainage Bag Volume

Background:
Urine bag monitoring in hospitals has not yet been incorporated into the digital management system.
The needs include:

Reporting the urine volume on the hour.

Early warning and alarm when the bag is nearly full or full.

Each room has approximately 25 beds.

Selected Design Solution:
Sub-1GHz (LoRaWAN) Weighing Node + Floor-level Gateway.

A LoRaWAN weighing device is installed at each bed.
It includes:

A low-power E-Ink display showing the current urine volume

A zero-setting button (also used as the start button)

Battery power supply (AA or Li-SoCl2)

An electronic weighing (pressure) sensor with 24-bit ADC

A hook for hanging the urine bag

A mechanical clamp for fixation to bed rails

The floor-level gateway includes:

A LoRaWAN Concentrator (Sub-1GHz)

Ethernet/Wi-Fi/Cellular connectivity to the Hospital Network

Capacity to handle an entire ward (25-100+ devices)
